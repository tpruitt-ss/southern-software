We Believe

How to Become
a Christian

At Southern Software, our faith in Jesus Christ is the foundation of everything we do. We share this because we believe it is the most important message we can offer.

Have you ever wondered what a Christian really is? The term is thrown around casually, but accepting Jesus Christ will change both your present life and your eternal destiny. It is the single most important decision you will ever make. Salvation begins with God, because of His great love for us.

Human Efforts Are Futile

God desires an intimate relationship with us, but we cannot obtain it through our own efforts. It is impossible for us to find peace with God on our own because our sin separates us from Him.

Romans 3:23 — "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (NIV)

A Gift From God

Salvation is a gift from God. He offers the gift through Jesus, His Son. By laying down His life on the cross, Christ took our place and paid the ultimate price for our sin — death. Jesus is our only way to God.

Romans 5:8 — "But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." (NIV)

How Do I Become a Christian?

1

Admit & Repent

Admit you are a sinner and turn away from your sin. Repent literally means "a change of mind that results in a change of action." Acts 3:19: "Repent, then, and turn to God, so that your sins may be wiped out." (NIV)

2

Believe

Believe Jesus Christ died on the cross to save you from your sins and give you eternal life. Romans 10:9-10: "If you declare with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." (NIV)

3

Pray

You may pray a simple prayer to God. Prayer is communicating with God — pray using your own words. There is no special formula. Just pray from your heart to God and believe that He has saved you.

4

Go Tell Someone

Find a group of like-minded people — a church — so you can grow in your faith. Hebrews 10:24-25: "And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds, not giving up meeting together." (NIV)

We Can Help

If you have questions about faith or would like someone to pray with you, we would love to hear from you.
